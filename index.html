The major firms surveyed thousands of executives. The data is useful at the macro level. But it describes the problem from 30,000 feet. None of it captures what is happening on the ground: why a pilot succeeds in one team and dies when it crosses a department boundary. Why the org chart hasn’t changed even though leadership says AI is the top priority. Why the most sophisticated AI usage in some companies is happening in the shadow, outside the official strategy.

These are not technology questions. They are organizational questions. And they require a different kind of research: structured conversations with the people who are knee deep building the tacit knowledge that surveys don’t reach.

We study two populations in parallel. Enterprise leaders restructuring organizations that were built before agents existed. And frontier operators who started with agents from day one: teams of 12 running at the output of 200, solo founders with agent departments, protocol teams building autonomous systems.

The enterprise side shows where transformation stalls under institutional weight. The frontier side shows what it looks like when it works. Together, the patterns tell a story neither population can see alone.

The Research

Six questions the surveys never asked.

01
How far has your organization actually restructured?
Not adopted tools. Restructured. Which roles changed? Which decision rights shifted? Which parts of the org chart haven’t moved at all? The distance between where you are and where the structure needs to be is the gap that determines everything else.
02
Where exactly does transformation break?
“Organizational failure” is a label, not a diagnosis. We’re distinguishing between culture failures (people feel threatened), structural failures (nobody owns the pilot-to-production path), governance failures (the “go fast but also slow down” contradiction), and political failures (quiet sabotage from people protecting territory). Each one requires a different response.
03
Where does the money actually go?
Research shows 70% of transformation value comes from changing how people work. Most organizations spend less than 15% of their AI budget there. The gap between where the money goes and where the value comes from is a pattern we can measure and benchmark across the cohort.
04
What’s happening below the radar?
In many organizations, the most sophisticated AI use is unsanctioned. Personal subscriptions. Workaround workflows. Tools that IT doesn’t know about. The shadow isn’t a compliance problem. It’s a signal about where the official strategy is failing to meet real needs.
05
Who wins and who loses inside the organization?
Restructuring redistributes power. Some roles expand, some disappear, some fight back quietly. The political dynamics of transformation are invisible in survey data. The interviews surface them.
06
Are regulatory deadlines helping or hurting?
New rules are arriving on a fixed timeline. For some organizations, the deadlines are creating the urgency that gets transformation funded. For others, compliance work is consuming all the oxygen. We want to know which pattern holds for you and why.
Context

New rules are arriving. Here’s what they mean for you specifically.

Whether or not regulation is your primary concern, the timeline below is reshaping the playing field for every organization that uses AI in production. It’s worth understanding why.

The short version: The EU has passed a set of laws that, for the first time, regulate how AI systems are built, governed, and audited inside organizations. If your company operates in Europe, serves European customers, or has European partners or suppliers, these apply to you. If you’re entirely US-based, they don’t apply directly today, but they are setting the standard that other jurisdictions are watching closely.

Why this matters even if you’re not directly regulated: Your European customers and partners will increasingly require evidence of AI governance from their suppliers. The organizational capabilities these rules demand (audit trails, human oversight frameworks, risk documentation) are the same capabilities that make AI transformation work at scale, regulation or not. And the companies that build this infrastructure now are building a structural advantage.
